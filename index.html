Threshold voltage

(Part 1 in a series of posts on small-form-factor computing)

The word threshold gets used a lot, especially in computing and technology. It doesn’t always mean the same thing. Let’s examine, non-academically, what it means in some contexts, and particularly how it applies to mainstream computing.

Device manufacturers know this. They design their products and integrate these chips without exceeding a certain thermal dissipation rate. This number is known in geek parlance as TDP—The Thermal Design Power, sometimes called thermal design point, is the maximum amount of heat generated by the CPU that the cooling system in a computer is required to dissipate in typical operation. For tablets, the TDP threshold is about 8W. It is why a 10W adapter is sufficient for just about any passively cooled tablet.
